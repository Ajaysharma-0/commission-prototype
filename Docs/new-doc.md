NIR Engine
Non-Inventory Revenue — Developer Specification
Internal — Confidential — For Development Team
1. Overview
NIR (Non-Inventory Revenue) is a passive revenue system for direct partner hotels. When a
guest stays at a partner hotel, that hotel claims a “socket” on the guest's account. From then on,
the hotel earns a share of revenue generated by that guest's future bookings across the entire
platform — not just at the hotel's own property.
Each guest account has exactly three sockets, labelled A, B, and C, each worth a different
earning percentage. Sockets are claimed in order by the partner hotels a guest successfully
stays at. The system tracks these connections (called “seeds”), calculates the revenue each
socket earns, and manages payouts.
Throughout this document: a “seed” is a single socket a hotel holds on a single guest. A hotel with
100,000 seeds holds a socket on 100,000 different guests. “Socket” and “slot” are used
interchangeably.
2. The NIR Formula
Every qualifying booking generates an NIR pool (internally called the “Safety Net”). The pool is
calculated as:
NIR Pool = (Travacot Net Revenue − Transaction Fee %) ÷ 2
The transaction fee percentage is a manually configurable value set by Travacot. It is subtracted
from Travacot's net revenue on the booking before the result is halved.
Each socket holder then earns their socket's percentage of that pool:
Socket Earning Rate Notes
Socket A 7.5% of the NIR pool Highest value — first claimed
Socket B 5% of the NIR pool Second claimed
Socket C 2.5% of the NIR pool Third claimed
Worked example: A $100 booking. Travacot's net revenue is $11. Assume the manual
transaction fee is set to a value leaving the full $11 in scope. NIR Pool = $11 ÷ 2 = $5.50.
Socket A earns 7.5% of $5.50 = $0.41; Socket B earns 5% = $0.28; Socket C earns 2.5% =
$0.14.
3. What Generates NIR
Normal bookings only
NIR is generated by normal bookings only. Stayva resale purchases never generate NIR. When
a transaction is a Stayva purchase, the NIR engine must skip it entirely.
The pool is generated platform-wide
The NIR pool is generated by a guest's bookings anywhere on the platform — including
bookings at hotels that are NOT direct partners. A non-partner hotel's booking still produces a
pool that the guest's socket-holding partner hotels earn from.
Critical distinction: Non-partner hotels generate pool revenue but can never hold a socket.
Only direct partner hotels can claim and hold sockets. A guest can book at fifty non-partner
hotels and generate fifty pools — all of which are earned by whichever partner hotels hold that
guest's three sockets.
4. Socket Assignment
How a socket is claimed
A partner hotel claims a socket on a guest when that guest completes a successful check-in and
check-out at the partner hotel. The claim happens on completion of the stay, not at booking
time.
Order of assignment
1. The first partner hotel a guest successfully stays at claims Socket A (7.5%).
2. The second different partner hotel claims Socket B (5%).
3. The third different partner hotel claims Socket C (2.5%).
Core rules
• One socket per hotel per guest. A hotel can hold at most one socket on any given guest.
• A repeat booking at a hotel that already holds a socket for that guest does NOT grant a
second socket. The hotel keeps its existing socket only.
• Once all three sockets are filled, any new partner hotel the guest stays at receives nothing
for that guest. The new partner must fill sockets on other guests instead.
• Non-partner hotels never claim sockets, regardless of how many times the guest stays
with them.
5. Self-Exclusion Rule
A socket-holding hotel earns NIR from the guest's bookings everywhere EXCEPT at its own
property.
The reasoning: when a guest books a repeat stay at a hotel that already holds their socket, that
hotel already profits directly from the stay. It therefore does not also earn NIR from that
particular booking.
Example: Hotel X holds Socket A on a guest. The guest later books at Hotel Y and Hotel Z
(other properties). Hotel X earns NIR from the Y and Z bookings. But if the guest books at Hotel
X again, Hotel X earns nothing in NIR from that booking — it already earns its normal revenue
from the direct stay.
Implementation: when calculating NIR for a booking, exclude any socket holder whose property is the
same as the property being booked. The other socket holders still earn normally from that booking's
pool.
6. Partner Departure & Socket Reshuffle
When a partner hotel leaves the partnership (ends the relationship, changes ownership, exits a
chain, etc.), all sockets it holds across all guests are emptied. The freed sockets do not stay
vacant in place — the lower sockets compact upward to fill the gap.
Reshuffle logic (per affected guest)
Vacated Socket What Happens Result
Socket A B moves up to A, C moves up to B C becomes empty
Socket B C moves up to B C becomes empty
Socket C Nothing moves C becomes empty
Promotion: Because socket percentages are tied to the slot position (not the hotel), a hotel that
moves up earns more. A hotel sitting in Socket C (2.5%) that gets promoted to Socket B now
earns 5%; promoted to Socket A it earns 7.5%. Surviving partners climb into higher-earning
positions as hotels above them leave.
The newly-empty bottom socket becomes claimable again by the next partner hotel the guest
successfully stays at.
This reshuffle is mandatory and must run automatically whenever a socket is vacated for any reason
(departure OR decay — see Section 7). Do not leave a hole in the socket order; always compact
upward.
7. Seed Decay (4-Year Reset)
To keep the system balanced and to reward deeper partner commitment, a hotel's total seed
count decays on a recurring cycle. Decay reduces the NUMBER of seeds a hotel holds — not
the earning rate of individual seeds.
Cadence
The decay sweep runs once every 4 years, per partner. Each partner has a single anchor
date (when they joined / claimed their first seed). Every 4 years from that anchor, the system
takes a snapshot of the partner's current total seed count and removes a percentage.
Use a single per-partner sweep, NOT per-seed timers. Do not give each seed its own 4-year clock. At
each 4-year mark, calculate the partner's total seeds and remove the percentage in one operation.
This is simpler to build and matches the snapshot model.
Decay rate
The decay rate depends on the partner's type. There are two distinct models: a fixed rate for
supplier-fed partners, and a commission-linked rate for directly integrated partners.
Supplier-fed partners (RateHawk) — fixed
Partners whose inventory is fed from RateHawk decay at a flat 7% every 4 years. This rate is
fixed. They do not get a commission choice and do not access the sliding scale, because their
inventory is not natively integrated into Travacot.
Partner Type Decay Rate Commission Choice
Supplier-fed (RateHawk) 7% every 4 years (fixed) None — not available
Directly integrated partners — commission-linked
Partners whose inventory lives natively inside Travacot's ecosystem choose a commission rate,
and that choice determines their decay rate. The higher the commission they give Travacot, the
lower their decay — they keep more of their seeds. These are fixed choices; partners select
from the options below and cannot enter a custom commission value. There is no commission
option below 10%.
Commission Choice Decay Every 4 Years
10% 5%
15% 2.5%
17.5% 1% (floor — cannot go lower)
Notes on the integrated model:
• These three are the only selectable options. No custom or in-between values (e.g. 12%,
16%) are allowed.
• There is no commission below 10%. 10% is the minimum.
• 1% is the hard floor on decay. No commission option pushes decay below 1%.
• Even the worst integrated rate (5% decay at 10% commission) is better than the supplierfed fixed rate (7%). Integration always pays off, and higher commission improves it
further.
This stacks two incentives: (1) integrate natively to unlock the sliding scale instead of being stuck at
the fixed 7%, and (2) within the scale, give higher commission for lower decay. Both pulls push
partners toward deeper commitment and better terms for Travacot.
When a commission change takes effect
A directly integrated partner can change their commission choice at any time, but the change
does NOT apply to the cycle currently in progress. The current 4-year cycle always completes
on the rate that was active when it began. The new rate applies starting from the NEXT cycle.
Worked example: A partner is at 10% commission (5% decay). Partway through their 4-year
cycle they switch to 15% commission (2.5% decay). At the end of the current cycle, the sweep
still removes 5% of their seeds — the rate the cycle started on. After that sweep, the new rate
takes over: the following cycle uses 2.5%, and they are treated as a 15% commission partner
going forward.
IMPLEMENTATION: store the decay rate that applies to the ACTIVE cycle separately from the
partner's currently-selected commission rate. A mid-cycle commission change updates the selected
rate but must NOT alter the active cycle's rate. When a sweep runs, it uses the active-cycle rate;
immediately after, the active-cycle rate is refreshed from the partner's current selection for the next
cycle. This prevents a partner from dodging a pending sweep by upgrading at the last minute.
Which seeds are removed first
When the sweep removes the percentage, it removes the LOWEST-VALUE seeds first.
Remove Socket C seeds first, then Socket B, and only touch Socket A seeds if the percentage
is large enough to exhaust everything below.
This naturally protects a hotel's most valuable connections — their Socket A relationships
survive longest — without needing a separate rule or timer. The protection emerges from the
removal order.
Reshuffle applies on decay
When decay empties a seed in a given guest's socket order, the same upward-compaction
reshuffle from Section 6 applies for that guest: lower sockets climb up, the bottom socket opens
for future claiming.
8. Partner Dashboard
Each partner sees a dashboard showing their NIR status. It must display:
Field Description
Account Type Corporate or Property Owner (set during onboarding)
Linked Properties Number of properties linked to this partner account
Connected Guests (Seeds) Total count of seeds the partner holds — number only, no guest personal data
NIR Pending Earned but not yet cleared for withdrawal
NIR Available Cleared and ready to cash out
Cash Out Action to withdraw NIR Available balance
The dashboard must NEVER expose guest personal data. Connected guests are shown as a count
only. The NIR engine tracks seeds by internal reference, never by exposing guest identities to
partners.
NIR Pending vs NIR Available mirrors a standard payments ledger (like Airbnb or Stripe): money is
earned into Pending, clears to Available on a schedule, and is withdrawn from Available via Cash Out.
9. Privacy & Data Handling
• The NIR engine tracks seeds and sockets by internal IDs only. Guest personal data is
never exposed to partner hotels.
• Partners see counts and revenue figures — never names, emails, or booking details of
the guests behind their seeds.
• Socket assignment, pool calculation, and decay all operate on internal references.
